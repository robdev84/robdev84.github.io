What is the Foundation for Fire and Life Safety?

The Foundation for Fire and Life Safety is an independent, not-for-profit 501(c)(3) organization dedicated to supporting community projects that improve fire safety and awareness in the community. The Foundation works closely with the West Chicago Fire Protection District.

Is the Foundation the same as the fire department?

No, the Foundation is a separate organization with it's own Board of Directors.

Where is the Foundation active?

The foundation is active in the West Chicago Fire Protection District. (See Google Map)

What is the mission of the Foundation?

The mission of the Foundation for Fire and Life Safety is to enhance public safety by improving educational outreach and material resources available to first responders and community organizations.

What are past, present, and future projects?

Current Project

The Foundation is currently working to install high visibility address signs for roughly 1,800 unincorporated addresses. If you would like to sign up for your free unincorporated address sign, click here:

Previous Projects

  • Installed lightning detection systems at a number of parks that notify patrons ahead of dangerous lightning strikes.
  • Provided AED's (Automatic External Defibrillators) to local churches and community locations.
  • Supports the Fire Safety trailor which the fire department uses to educate at the elementary schools.
  • Purchased cooling chairs that provide relief from high temperatures.
  • Provides overnight hotel stays for victims of fires.
  • Offers scholarships for high school students who are interested in pursuing careers in fire technology.
  • Purchased AHA training dummies for CPR instruction.

Future Projects

The Foundation would like to purchase a fire extinguisher simulator that trains the proper technique for putting out a fire with a handheld fire extinguisher for use in community schools and businesses.
